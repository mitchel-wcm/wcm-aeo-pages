01 / Context

Why Old Hickory Lake demands a capable truck

Short answer Old Hickory Lake is a 23,000-acre reservoir with 440 miles of shoreline and some of the best striped bass, largemouth, crappie, and catfish fishing in Tennessee — but getting a boat in and out requires a truck that can handle loaded trailers, uneven ramp surfaces, and the occasional muddy parking area.

For Wilson County residents, Old Hickory Lake isn't just a fishing hole — it's a way of life. Whether you're launching a 16-foot aluminum bass boat before sunrise or backing a 24-foot pontoon down for a summer afternoon with the family, your truck needs to do more than just move the rig. It needs to back straight, hold steady on the ramp incline, and make the whole process feel effortless.

The 2026 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 was built with exactly these situations in mind. With available camera systems for hitch guidance, an in-vehicle trailering app, and engine options rated up to 13,300 lbs of towing capacity, it's among the most capable half-ton trucks Wilson County anglers can buy — and it's sitting on the lot right here in Lebanon.

Percy Priest Lake adds another dimension. Located about 35 minutes southwest of Lebanon near Smyrna and La Vergne, Percy Priest is famous for its crappie and hybrid bass fishery. Many Wilson County families keep boats for both lakes — which means your truck needs to be equally at home on the smooth concrete ramps at Seven Points and the gravel entry at Defeated Creek Marina on Old Hickory.

06 / Walkthrough

How to back a boat trailer with a Silverado

Short answer Using the Silverado's Advanced Trailering System, backing a boat trailer is a five-step process: pre-launch check, engage 4WD and Tow/Haul, line up with the ramp, back with Hitch View, and hold with Hill Start Assist.

  1. Pre-launch check

    From the in-vehicle Trailering App, verify trailer tire pressure, running lights, and brake controller gain before leaving the staging lane.

  2. Engage 4WD and Tow/Haul

    Shift to 4WD Auto (or 4WD High on gravel ramps) and select Tow/Haul mode to sharpen throttle response and hold lower gears.

  3. Line up with the ramp

    Square the truck to the ramp lane. Use the 15-view camera system to check clearance from trailers in adjacent lanes.

  4. Back with Hitch View

    Switch to the rearview camera with trailering overlay. Steer slowly — small corrections — until the trailer's bunks reach the right depth for your boat.

  5. Hold with Hill Start Assist

    Hill Start Assist prevents rollback when you shift to park. Set the parking brake, then release your boat.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions from Wilson County anglers and boaters about the 2026 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 and boat towing in Middle Tennessee.

What is the towing capacity of the 2026 Chevrolet Silverado 1500?
The 2026 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 can tow up to 13,300 pounds when equipped with the 6.2L V8 engine, 4WD, and the Max Trailering Package. The 2.7L TurboMax tows up to 9,500 lbs and the 5.3L V8 tows up to 11,500 lbs — more than enough for any bass boat, pontoon, or fishing rig used on Old Hickory Lake and Percy Priest Lake.
Can a Chevrolet Silverado 1500 tow a pontoon boat?
Yes. Most pontoon boats weigh between 2,500 and 5,500 pounds with trailer — well within the Silverado 1500's range. A 20-foot pontoon with a 150-hp outboard and loaded trailer typically weighs around 4,200 lbs. The Silverado 1500 handles this comfortably with any engine option, including the base 2.7L TurboMax.
Which Silverado 1500 trim is best for towing a boat on Old Hickory Lake?
For most boaters, the Silverado LTZ with the 5.3L V8 and Max Trailering Package is the best value — 11,500 lbs towing, standard Advanced Trailering System, Hitch View camera, and a refined interior. If you fish muddy or rough ramps, the LT Trail Boss adds a 2-inch lift, all-terrain tires, and an automatic locking rear differential at a lower price.
Do I need a Silverado 1500 or Silverado HD for my boat?
For 95% of recreational boaters in Middle Tennessee, the Silverado 1500 is the right truck. HD models (2500, 3500) are needed only when your loaded boat-and-trailer combination exceeds roughly 13,000–14,000 lbs. Bass boats, pontoons, wake boats, and ski boats used on Old Hickory and Percy Priest all fall well below that threshold. The 1500 delivers better fuel economy, a more comfortable ride, and is easier to maneuver in marina parking areas.
Does the Silverado 1500 have a camera for backing a boat trailer?
Yes. The available Advanced Trailering System includes Hitch Guidance with Hitch View — a dedicated camera view that shows your hitch ball and trailer coupler with a guidance overlay. The system also offers up to 15 total camera views, including a trailer-end camera and bird's-eye perspective, making it significantly easier to back down narrow ramps at busy boat launches.
