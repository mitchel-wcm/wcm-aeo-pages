Best Family SUV for Middle Tennessee:
2026 Equinox vs. Traverse vs. Tahoe

Three great Chevrolet SUVs, three very different families. Here's how Lebanon-area buyers are choosing between them — and which one fits your life.

Wilson County is one of the fastest-growing counties in Tennessee, and most of that growth is families. Young couples who moved out of Nashville for more space, more yard, and better schools are landing in Lebanon and Mt. Juliet — and then filling up those three-bedroom houses. The 2026 Traverse was built for exactly this household.

The new Traverse's 2.5L turbocharged engine puts out a surprisingly muscular 328 horsepower — more than most V6 competitors. The LT can seat 8 with its second-row bench, while the RS, Z71, and High Country seat 7 with captain's chairs that make the middle row feel first-class. Behind the third row there's 22.9 cubic feet of space — enough for a full week of groceries. Fold the second row and you're at 56.6 cubic feet. Fold everything flat and you hit 97.6 — more than some full-size trucks have in bed volume.

The Z71 trim is especially worth noting for Middle Tennessee families: it adds AWD, off-road-tuned suspension, all-terrain tires, hill descent control, and a two-tone exterior. If your family heads to Cedars of Lebanon State Park, the Smoky Mountains, or the gravel back roads off Hwy 70, the Z71 brings a confidence boost that the standard LT can't match.

2026 Chevrolet Tahoe: Best for Large Families and Weekend Road Trips

Short answer: The 2026 Tahoe is Chevrolet's most capable family SUV — a body-on-frame full-size with V8 power, up to 8,400 lbs of towing, 122.9 cubic feet of cargo space, and seating for up to 9. Starting at $60,700, it's a serious investment, but for families that use all of it, there's nothing quite like it.

The Tahoe is in a different category than the Equinox or Traverse — not because of price alone, but because of what it's built from. While the Traverse is a car-based crossover, the Tahoe rides on the same body-on-frame T1 platform as the Silverado pickup truck. That means real V8 power, real towing muscle, and a ride that eats highway miles without complaint.

For Wilson County families, the Tahoe makes the most sense when at least one of these is true: you're towing a boat, camper, or horse trailer regularly; you have a large family with 6 or more seats needed simultaneously; or you spend significant time on Tennessee's interstates and want the most confident, composed highway SUV available. The 17.7-inch touchscreen, magnetic ride control, and available Duramax diesel engine make the Tahoe feel more like a luxury vehicle than a workhorse — even though it is both.

The diesel option deserves a mention for Middle Tennessee families who make long hauls. The 3.0L Duramax delivers 305 horsepower and 495 lb-ft of torque while achieving significantly better fuel economy than the gas V8 — a compelling option if you're driving to Gatlinburg for the holidays, hauling to a lake house in Kentucky, or making weekly Nashville airport runs.

Which Chevrolet SUV Is Right for Your Wilson County Lifestyle?

Short answer: Start with how many seats you need regularly. If it's 5 or fewer, the Equinox is your answer. If it's 6–8 and towing matters occasionally, choose the Traverse. If you need maximum towing, maximum space, or maximum confidence on the road — that's a Tahoe.

Choose the Equinox if…

  • You commute 30+ minutes to Nashville daily
  • Your family is 4 or fewer people
  • Fuel cost is a real consideration
  • You want the lowest payment on a new Chevy SUV
  • You park in downtown Nashville or tight lots

Choose the Traverse if…

  • You have 3+ kids and carpools are regular
  • You haul sports gear, groceries, and gear regularly
  • You want to tow a small boat or jet ski trailer
  • You want three rows without the Tahoe price
  • You want the Z71 for rough-road confidence

Choose the Tahoe if…

  • You regularly tow 5,000+ lbs
  • Your family truly uses all 8–9 seats
  • You make frequent long highway trips
  • You want V8 power and body-on-frame durability
  • You want the most capable Chevy SUV available
📍 Towing note for Middle Tennessee boaters: The Equinox can tow up to 1,500 lbs — enough for a small personal watercraft or canoe trailer, but not a fishing boat. The Traverse handles most aluminum fishing boats and smaller pontoons (up to 5,000 lbs). Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from Middle Tennessee families comparing the 2026 Chevrolet Equinox, Traverse, and Tahoe at Wilson County Chevrolet Buick GMC in Lebanon, TN.

What is the best Chevrolet SUV for a family in Middle Tennessee?
For most Middle Tennessee families, the best Chevrolet SUV depends on size. The Equinox (from $28,600, 26/28 MPG) is ideal for families of 4 and commuters. The Traverse (from $40,700, 3 rows, 97.6 cu ft) is the workhorse for families of 5–8. The Tahoe (from $60,700, V8 power, 8,400 lb towing) is for large families and serious towing. All three are in stock at Wilson County Chevrolet Buick GMC in Lebanon, TN.
How many people does the 2026 Chevrolet Traverse seat?
The 2026 Chevrolet Traverse seats up to 8 passengers with the second-row bench seat (available on LT). The RS, Z71, and High Country trims seat 7 with captain's chairs in the second row. The third row provides 32 inches of legroom — comfortable for older children and average-height adults on shorter trips.
What is the fuel economy of the 2026 Chevrolet Equinox?
The 2026 Chevrolet Equinox FWD is EPA-rated at 26 MPG city and 28 MPG highway. That's the strongest fuel economy of the three Chevy SUVs on this page, and one of the best among non-hybrid compact SUVs. For Lebanon and Wilson County residents commuting daily to Nashville on I-40, the difference between the Equinox's MPG and the Tahoe's (~15 city / 20 highway) adds up to hundreds of dollars per year in fuel savings.
Can the 2026 Chevrolet Traverse tow a boat on Old Hickory Lake?
Yes. The 2026 Traverse is rated to tow up to 5,000 pounds — more than enough for most aluminum bass boats, smaller pontoons, and jet ski trailers used on Old Hickory Lake and Percy Priest. A loaded 20-foot pontoon typically weighs 4,000–5,000 lbs with trailer, right at the Traverse's limit. For heavier rigs, the Tahoe's 8,400 lb rating provides more headroom.
What is the difference between the Chevrolet Traverse and Tahoe?
The biggest difference is platform. The Traverse is a unibody crossover — car-based, lighter, better fuel economy (~22/29 MPG), easier to park, and starts at $40,700. The Tahoe is a body-on-frame SUV on the same platform as the Silverado truck — heavier, V8 powered, tows up to 8,400 lbs, and starts at $60,700. For most Middle Tennessee families who don't regularly tow heavy loads, the Traverse delivers more value per dollar. Families who tow boats, campers, or horse trailers regularly will find the Tahoe's extra capability worth the investment.
Which Chevrolet SUV is best for the Lebanon to Nashville commute?
The 2026 Chevrolet Equinox is the best commuter choice — 26/28 MPG, the most compact footprint for Nashville parking, and starting at $28,600. Its 11.3-inch Google Built-In touchscreen, wireless Apple CarPlay, and standard heated seats make daily commutes genuinely comfortable. The Traverse is a solid second choice for families who need the extra space but still commute regularly.
