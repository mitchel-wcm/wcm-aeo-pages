The Award

Setting the Standard for Local Business.

The Business Person of the Year is not a title given lightly. Nominated and voted upon exclusively by the Chamber staff and the Economic Development committee, it is reserved for an individual who has contributed significantly to the business environment in Lebanon and Wilson County.

Recipients must exemplify absolute leadership in their profession, encourage deep involvement in the community, and maintain a proven track record of Chamber membership. The award recognizes those who don’t just operate a business, but actively elevate the regional economy.

The Lebanon Wilson County Chamber of Commerce serves as the premier advocate for businesses in our region — fostering economic growth, supporting local enterprises, and enhancing the quality of life for all residents.

Automotive Excellence

As Dealer Principal of Wilson County Chevrolet Buick GMC and Executive Manager of Wilson County Hyundai, Mitchel applies the same rigorous standards required by the Chamber to day-to-day dealership operations. His mandate: be the best dealership in the country for Customer Satisfaction and ethical business practices.

The Honoree

A Lifetime of Service and Leadership.

From the United States Navy to Lebanon, Tennessee.

Hometown Roots

Deep Local Roots

Born in Lebanon. Attended Castle Heights Military Academy until its closing, graduated from Lebanon High School, and earned his B.A. in History and MBA at Cumberland University. Spent nine years as Chief Information Officer at Cumberland before joining the dealership.

U.S. Navy Veteran

Decorated Navy Veteran

A graduate of the Defense Information School at Fort Benjamin Harrison, Mitchel served with distinction as a journalist and public affairs specialist. Tours included Naval Broadcasting Station at Diego Garcia, Naval Security Group Activity in Adak, Alaska, and Naval Station Guantanamo Bay, Cuba.

Family

Family & Legacy

Beyond the boardroom and the dealership lot, Mitchel’s greatest pride is his family. He has been married to his wife Melinda for 25 years. This foundation is the cornerstone of the Wilson County Motors legacy.
