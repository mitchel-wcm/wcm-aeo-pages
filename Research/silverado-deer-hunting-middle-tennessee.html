03 / Payload

What can you actually haul in a Silverado bed on hunting day?

Short answer The 2026 Silverado 1500 offers up to 2,280 lbs of payload. A typical Wilson County hunting load — one 180-lb dressed deer, two 50-lb corn feeders, a protein feeder, a climbing stand, a ground blind, and a full gear bag — runs roughly 450 lbs. That leaves over 1,700 lbs of remaining capacity for additional equipment, passengers, or another trip's worth of gear.

Bed length guide for hunters

  • 5'8" short bed — Fine for most gear loads and a single deer. Tight for full-size 16' ladder stands; you'll need a bed extender.
  • 6'6" standard bed — The hunting sweet spot. Deer lays flat, two stands side by side, box blind crate with room for gear. Recommended for most Wilson County hunters.
  • 8'2" long bed — Maximum capacity. Bulk corn, stands, a box blind, and a full gear load simultaneously. Best if the truck also sees farm or ranch work.

05 / Off-Road

4WD & off-road field access in Middle Tennessee

Short answer The Trail Boss is the most capable factory half-ton for off-road field access without aftermarket mods. Its 2-inch lift, locking rear diff, 33-inch Duratrac tires, and Terrain Management with dedicated Off-Road mode handle the wet October clay and steep creek-bank entry common to Wilson, Smith, Trousdale, and DeKalb County hunting ground.

06 / Season

When is deer season in Wilson County, Tennessee?

Short answer Wilson County is in Tennessee Zone L. Archery typically opens late September and runs through early January. Muzzleloader runs in October and again in December. Modern gun opens early-to-mid November with concurrent archery. Always verify current-year dates with TWRA before buying tags.
